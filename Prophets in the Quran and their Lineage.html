Prophets in the Quran and their Lineage

An interactive flowchart illustrating the genealogical connections of revered Prophets mentioned in the Holy Quran, along with brief mentions of significant Quranic stories.

Prophetic Lineage Flowchart

Adam (AS) - آدم

The first human and prophet, directly created by Allah from clay.

Quranic Story: His creation, prostration of angels, dwelling in Paradise, eating from the forbidden tree, seeking forgiveness, and being sent to Earth (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:30-39).

Shith (Seth)

Son of Adam (AS). Continued the message of monotheism.

Idris (AS) - أخنخ (Enoch)

Descendant of Shith. Known for his wisdom and being elevated to a high station.

Quranic Story: Mentioned as truthful and a prophet, elevated to a high position (Surah Maryam 19:56-57).

Nuh (AS) - نوح (Noah)

Nuh ibn Lamik ibn Muttashalakh ibn Akhnukh (Idris).

Quranic Story: Preached monotheism for centuries, built the Ark, the Great Flood (Surah Nuh, Surah Hud 11:25-49).

Sam

Son of Nuh (AS). From his lineage came several prophets.

Ibrahim (AS) - إبراهيم (Abraham)

Ibrahim ibn Tarah ibn Nahur ... ibn Sam ibn Nuh (AS).

Quranic Story: His struggle against idol worship, dream of sacrificing Isma'il, building the Kaaba (Surah Al-Anbiya 21:51-73, Surah As-Saffat 37:99-113).

Isma'il (AS) - إسماعيل (Ishmael)

Son of Ibrahim (AS). Ancestor of Prophet Muhammad (SAW).

Quranic Story: Assisted Ibrahim in building the Kaaba, nearly sacrificed (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:127, Surah As-Saffat 37:102).

Muhammad (SAW) - محمد

Muhammad ibn Abdullah ... ibn Adnan (descendant of Isma'il AS).

Quranic Story: Revelation of the Quran, Hijra, battles of Badr and Uhud, conquest of Makkah, establishing Islam (Numerous Surahs, e.g., Surah Al-Fath 48, Surah Al-Ahzab 33).

Ishaq (AS) - إسحاق (Isaac)

Son of Ibrahim (AS). Ancestor of many Israelite prophets.

Quranic Story: His birth was a glad tiding to Ibrahim and Sarah in their old age (Surah Hud 11:71).

Ya'qub (AS) - يعقوب (Jacob/Israel)

Son of Ishaq (AS). Also known as Israel, father of the 12 tribes.

Quranic Story: His covenant with Allah, his sons, and his vision for his progeny (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:132-133, Surah Yusuf 12).

Yusuf (AS) - يوسف (Joseph)

Son of Ya'qub (AS).

Quranic Story: The entire Surah Yusuf details his life, from the well to his rise in Egypt (Surah Yusuf 12).

Lawi (Levi)

Son of Ya'qub (AS). From his lineage came Musa (AS).

Musa (AS) - موسى (Moses)

Musa ibn Imran ibn Qahath ibn Lawi ibn Ya'qub (AS).

Quranic Story: His birth, growing up in Pharaoh's palace, parting the Red Sea, receiving the Torah (Surah Taha 20, Surah Al-Qasas 28).

Harun (AS) - هارون (Aaron)

Brother of Musa (AS), also a prophet.

Quranic Story: Assisted Musa in his mission to Pharaoh, his eloquence (Surah Taha 20:29-36).

Dawud (AS) - داود (David)

Descendant of Ya'qub (AS). A prophet-king.

Quranic Story: Defeated Goliath, was given the Zabur (Psalms), wise judgment, softened iron (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:251, Surah Saba 34:10-11).

Sulaiman (AS) - سليمان (Solomon)

Son of Dawud (AS). A prophet-king with immense power.

Quranic Story: Controlled jinn, animals, and wind; visited Queen of Sheba (Surah An-Naml 27, Surah Saba 34:12-14).

Zakariyya (AS) - زكريا (Zechariah)

Descendant of Dawud (AS). Guardian of Maryam.

Quranic Story: Prayed for a child in old age, received Yahya (John) as a sign (Surah Ali 'Imran 3:37-41, Surah Maryam 19:2-15).

Yahya (AS) - يحيى (John the Baptist)

Son of Zakariyya (AS). Preceded Isa (AS).

Quranic Story: His birth was a miracle, described as noble, chaste, and a prophet (Surah Ali 'Imran 3:39, Surah Maryam 19:7-15).

Maryam (Mary)

From the lineage of Dawud (AS). Mother of Isa (AS).

Quranic Story: Her miraculous conception of Isa, her piety and devotion, the annunciation (Surah Maryam 19, Surah Ali 'Imran 3:42-47).

Isa (AS) - عيسى (Jesus)

Son of Maryam. No human father.

Quranic Story: Miraculous birth, speaking from the cradle, performing miracles by Allah's will, not crucified, raised to heavens (Surah Ali 'Imran 3:45-55, Surah An-Nisa 4:157-158).

`

This information is based on Islamic tradition and Quranic narrations. (AS) stands for 'Alayhis Salam' (Peace be upon him), and (SAW) stands for 'Sallallahu alayhi wa sallam' (May Allah's peace and blessings be upon him).
