سُورَةُ الْفَاتِحَة

Surah Al-Fatiha

"The Opening"

The Essence of the Quran

Surah Al-Fatiha is the first chapter of the Holy Quran. Its seven verses are a prayer for the guidance, lordship, and mercy of Allah. This Surah holds a special place in Islam and is a compulsory part of a Muslim's daily prayers. It serves as a concise summary of the entire Quranic message.

Context and Revelation

Classification: According to the vast majority of scholars, Surah Al-Fatiha is a Meccan (Makki) surah. This means it was revealed in Mecca before the Hijra (migration) to Medina.

Occasion of Revelation: Unlike many other surahs revealed in response to specific events, Al-Fatiha was revealed as a complete and foundational chapter. It was given to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) early in his mission to establish the core principles of faith and to serve as the cornerstone of Islamic worship (Salah). It is considered a direct dialogue between the Creator and the servant, teaching us how to praise, acknowledge, and supplicate to Him.

The Many Names of Al-Fatiha

The importance of this Surah is highlighted by the many names given to it, each describing a unique aspect of its greatness:
  • Umm Al-Kitab (The Mother of the Book): Because it contains the essence of the entire Quran's message.
  • As-Salah (The Prayer): As no prayer is complete without its recitation.
  • Ash-Shifa (The Cure): It is considered a source of spiritual and physical healing.
  • As-Sab' al-Mathani (The Seven Oft-Repeated Verses): Because its seven verses are repeated in every unit of prayer.
  • Al-Hamd (The Praise): As it begins with the ultimate praise of Allah.
1

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

Bismillaahir Rahmaanir Raheem

In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.

Explanation:

This verse, known as the Basmala, marks the beginning. It is a declaration that we start every action in the name of Allah, seeking His blessings and help. It reminds us of two of Allah's most significant attributes: Ar-Rahman (Mercy that encompasses all creation) and Ar-Rahim (Mercy that is specific to the believers).

2

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

Alhamdu lillaahi Rabbil 'aalameen

All praise is due to Allah, Lord of the worlds.

Explanation:

This is a statement of profound gratitude and praise. "Al-Hamd" signifies the most sincere and comprehensive praise for all the perfect attributes and blessings of Allah. By declaring Him "Rabbil 'alamin" (Lord of all worlds), we acknowledge His absolute sovereignty, authority, and sustenance over everything that exists—mankind, jinn, angels, and all of creation.

3

الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

Ar-Rahmaanir-Raheem

The Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.

Explanation:

The repetition of these names from the Basmala emphasizes the boundless and all-encompassing nature of Allah's mercy. It reassures the believer that the Lord they are praising is not a distant, unapproachable tyrant, but a God whose fundamental nature is mercy. This fosters both love and hope in the heart of the worshipper.

4

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

Maaliki Yawmid-Deen

Master of the Day of Judgment.

Explanation:

After establishing His mercy, this verse reminds us of His justice. Allah is the sole Owner and Judge on the Day of Recompense, when all deeds will be accounted for. This awareness instills a sense of accountability and encourages righteousness, balancing the hope derived from His mercy with a healthy fear of His justice.

5

إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ

Iyyaaka na'budu wa lyyaaka nasta'een

It is You we worship and You we ask for help.

Explanation:

This verse is the heart of the Surah and a central pillar of the Islamic faith. It is a direct address to Allah, a declaration of pure monotheism. We affirm that our worship—our prayers, our devotion, our entire lives—is for Him alone. We also affirm that we seek help and support from no one but Him, acknowledging our complete dependence on His power and grace.

6

اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

Ihdinas-Siraatal-Mustaqeem

Guide us to the straight path.

Explanation:

Having acknowledged Allah's greatness and our devotion to Him, we now make the most important request: guidance. "As-Sirat al-Mustaqim" is the clear, direct, and correct path that leads to Allah's pleasure and salvation. It is the path of truth, knowledge, and righteous action, as defined by the Quran and the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

7

صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

Siraatal-lazeena an'amta 'alaihim ghayril-maghdoobi 'alaihim wa lad-daalleen

The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have earned Your anger or of those who are astray.

Explanation:

This final verse further defines the "straight path." It is the path of the Prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous—those who received Allah's grace and favor. We ask to be protected from two other paths: the path of those who knew the truth but rejected it, thus earning Allah's anger, and the path of those who strayed from the truth due to ignorance or negligence.
